1 Basic Finance Terms

Core finance vocabulary you will see in class, business news, and everyday financial discussions.

Basic
Revenue
Chinese: 收入
Pinyin: shōu rù

Explanation: Revenue is the total money a business earns from selling products or services — before any expenses are deducted.

Example: The coffee shop's revenue comes from selling drinks and snacks.

中文例句: 這家咖啡店的收入來自賣飲料和點心。

zhè jiā kā fēi diàn de shōu rù lái zì mài yǐn liào hé diǎn xīn

Basic
Expense
Chinese: 支出
Pinyin: zhī chū

Explanation: An expense is money spent on things needed to run a business or daily life — such as rent, food, transport, or supplies.

Example: Rent is usually a major monthly expense for students in Taipei.

中文例句: 在台北，房租通常是學生每月很大的支出。

zài tái běi, fáng zū tōng cháng shì xué shēng měi yuè hěn dà de zhī chū

Basic
Profit
Chinese: 利潤
Pinyin: lì rùn

Explanation: Profit is what remains after all expenses are subtracted from revenue. Positive profit means the business earned more than it spent.

Example: If a store earns NT$10,000 and spends NT$7,000, its profit is NT$3,000.

中文例句: 如果商店收入一萬、支出七千，它的利潤就是三千。

rú guǒ shāng diàn shōu rù yí wàn, zhī chū qī qiān, tā de lì rùn jiù shì sān qiān

Basic
Asset
Chinese: 資產
Pinyin: zī chǎn

Explanation: An asset is anything valuable that a person or company owns — including cash, property, equipment, or investments.

Example: Cash in the bank and a company car are both assets.

中文例句: 銀行裡的現金和公司的車都算是資產。

yín háng lǐ de xiàn jīn hé gōng sī de chē dōu suàn shì zī chǎn

Basic
Liability
Chinese: 負債
Pinyin: fù zhài

Explanation: A liability is a financial obligation — money that a person or company owes to others, such as loans, credit card debt, or unpaid bills.

Example: A bank loan is a liability because the money must be paid back.

中文例句: 銀行貸款是一種負債，因為之後要還錢。

yín háng dài kuǎn shì yì zhǒng fù zhài, yīn wèi zhī hòu yào huán qián

Quick Memory: Revenue is money in, expense is money out, and profit is what stays after costs. Asset = what you own, Liability = what you owe.

2 Banking in Taiwan

Useful banking words for opening an account, using ATMs, and handling everyday money matters in Taiwan.

Banking
Bank Account
Chinese: 銀行帳戶
Pinyin: yín háng zhàng hù

Explanation: A bank account is a secure place at a financial institution where you keep and manage your money.

Example: Many foreign students open a bank account after getting their ARC in Taiwan.

中文例句: 很多外國學生拿到居留證後會開銀行帳戶。

hěn duō wài guó xué shēng ná dào jū liú zhèng hòu huì kāi yín háng zhàng hù

Banking
Deposit
Chinese: 存款
Pinyin: cún kuǎn

Explanation: A deposit means putting money into your bank account — either at the counter, via ATM, or through mobile banking.

Example: Some ATMs in Taiwan allow you to make deposits directly.

中文例句: 在台灣，有些提款機可以直接存款。

zài tái wān, yǒu xiē tí kuǎn jī kě yǐ zhí jiē cún kuǎn

Banking
Withdrawal
Chinese: 提款
Pinyin: tí kuǎn

Explanation: A withdrawal means taking money out of your bank account — at an ATM, bank counter, or app.

Example: I made a cash withdrawal before paying my rent.

中文例句: 我付房租前先去提款。

wǒ fù fáng zū qián xiān qù tí kuǎn

Banking
Transfer
Chinese: 轉帳
Pinyin: zhuǎn zhàng

Explanation: A transfer is electronically sending money from one bank account to another — common for paying rent, splitting bills, or sending money home.

Example: I transfer rent to my landlord every month.

中文例句: 我每個月都轉帳房租給房東。

wǒ měi gè yuè dōu zhuǎn zhàng fáng zū gěi fáng dōng

Banking
Balance
Chinese: 餘額
Pinyin: yú é

Explanation: Balance is the total amount of money currently in your bank account — what's left after deposits and withdrawals.

Example: You can check your balance using your banking app anytime.

中文例句: 你可以用手機銀行查看帳戶餘額。

nǐ kě yǐ yòng shǒu jī yín háng chá kàn zhàng hù yú é

Quick Memory: 存款 = money goes in, 提款 = money comes out, 轉帳 = money moves between accounts, 餘額 = what's left.

3 Income & Expenses

Daily budgeting words for salary, spending, and managing student life in Taiwan.

Income
Salary
Chinese: 薪水
Pinyin: xīn shuǐ

Explanation: Salary is the regular, fixed amount of money you receive from your employer in exchange for your work.

Example: My part-time salary is paid at the end of each month.

中文例句: 我的打工薪水在每個月底發。

wǒ de dǎ gōng xīn shuǐ zài měi gè yuè dǐ fā

Income
Budget
Chinese: 預算
Pinyin: yù suàn

Explanation: A budget is a financial plan that allocates your income toward different categories of spending and saving.

Example: I made a monthly budget for food, transport, and rent.

中文例句: 我做了每月的預算，分配食物、交通和房租。

wǒ zuò le měi yuè de yù suàn, fēn pèi shí wù, jiāo tōng hé fáng zū

Income
Living Expenses
Chinese: 生活費
Pinyin: shēng huó fèi

Explanation: Living expenses are the total costs of everyday life — including food, rent, transportation, utilities, and personal care.

Example: Living expenses in Taipei are often higher than in smaller cities.

中文例句: 台北的生活費通常比小城市高。

tái běi de shēng huó fèi tōng cháng bǐ xiǎo chéng shì gāo

Income
Savings
Chinese: 儲蓄
Pinyin: chǔ xù

Explanation: Savings is money you set aside and don't spend — kept for future goals, emergencies, or investments.

Example: I put part of my salary into savings every month.

中文例句: 我每個月都把一部分薪水存成儲蓄。

wǒ měi gè yuè dōu bǎ yí bù fèn xīn shuǐ cún chéng chǔ xù

Income
Discount
Chinese: 折扣
Pinyin: zhé kòu

Explanation: A discount is a reduction from the original price — often expressed as a percentage off.

Example: Students often look for discounts before buying textbooks.

中文例句: 學生買課本前常常會先找折扣。

xué shēng mǎi kè běn qián cháng cháng huì xiān zhǎo zhé kòu

Quick Memory: Salary gives you income, a budget guides spending, savings protect your future, and discounts reduce what you spend.

4 Financial Statements

Main accounting terms used to read and interpret company performance and financial health.

Statements
Balance Sheet
Chinese: 資產負債表
Pinyin: zī chǎn fù zhài biǎo

Explanation: A balance sheet is a financial snapshot showing a company's assets, liabilities, and equity at a specific point in time.

Example: Investors use the balance sheet to see what a company owns and owes.

中文例句: 投資人會看資產負債表來了解公司擁有什麼和欠什麼。

tóu zī rén huì kàn zī chǎn fù zhài biǎo lái liǎo jiě gōng sī yōng yǒu shén me hé qiàn shén me

Statements
Income Statement
Chinese: 損益表
Pinyin: sǔn yì biǎo

Explanation: An income statement (P&L) shows a company's revenue, expenses, and profit over a specific time period.

Example: We can use the income statement to see whether the company made money this year.

中文例句: 我們可以從損益表看出公司今年有沒有賺錢。

wǒ men kě yǐ cóng sǔn yì biǎo kàn chū gōng sī jīn nián yǒu méi yǒu zhuàn qián

Statements
Cash Flow Statement
Chinese: 現金流量表
Pinyin: xiàn jīn liú liàng biǎo

Explanation: A cash flow statement tracks how cash enters and leaves a company — covering operating, investing, and financing activities.

Example: A company can be profitable but still have weak cash flow.

中文例句: 公司可能有利潤，但現金流還是可能很弱。

gōng sī kě néng yǒu lì rùn, dàn xiàn jīn liú hái shì kě néng hěn ruò

Statements
Equity
Chinese: 權益
Pinyin: quán yì

Explanation: Equity (also called net assets) is the owner's residual interest — what's left when you subtract liabilities from total assets.

Example: If assets grow faster than liabilities, equity usually increases.

中文例句: 如果資產增加得比負債快，權益通常也會增加。

rú guǒ zī chǎn zēng jiā de bǐ fù zhài kuài, quán yì tōng cháng yě huì zēng jiā

Statements
Net Profit
Chinese: 淨利
Pinyin: jìng lì

Explanation: Net profit is the bottom-line result — revenue minus all expenses, interest, and taxes. It's the true measure of what the company earned.

Example: Investors care about net profit because it reflects the company's final earnings.

中文例句: 投資人很重視淨利，因為它代表公司的最後獲利。

tóu zī rén hěn zhòng shì jìng lì, yīn wèi tā dài biǎo gōng sī de zuì hòu huò lì

Quick Memory: Balance sheet = snapshot in time. Income statement = performance over time. Cash flow statement = real cash movement. Net profit = the final number.

5 Investing Basics

Starter vocabulary for understanding stocks, bonds, returns, risk, and building a portfolio.

Investing
Stock
Chinese: 股票
Pinyin: gǔ piào

Explanation: A stock represents partial ownership in a company. Stockholders may earn dividends and benefit from price appreciation.

Example: Buying a stock means you own a small part of that company.

中文例句: 買股票代表你擁有那家公司的一小部分。

mǎi gǔ piào dài biǎo nǐ yōng yǒu nà jiā gōng sī de yì xiǎo bù fèn

Investing
Bond
Chinese: 債券
Pinyin: zhài quàn

Explanation: A bond is a debt instrument — you lend money to a government or company, and they repay you with interest over a fixed period.

Example: People who invest in bonds usually receive regular interest payments.

中文例句: 投資債券的人通常會收到利息。

tóu zī zhài quàn de rén tōng cháng huì shōu dào lì xí

Investing
Risk
Chinese: 風險
Pinyin: fēng xiǎn

Explanation: Risk is the possibility that an investment will lose value or not perform as expected. Higher potential returns usually come with higher risk.

Example: Higher returns often come with higher risk.

中文例句: 比較高的報酬通常也有比較高的風險。

bǐ jiào gāo de bào chóu tōng cháng yě yǒu bǐ jiào gāo de fēng xiǎn

Investing
Return
Chinese: 報酬
Pinyin: bào chóu

Explanation: Return is the gain or loss generated from an investment — expressed as money or as a percentage of the amount invested.

Example: Good investments usually aim for stable long-term returns.

中文例句: 好的投資通常希望有穩定的長期報酬。

hǎo de tóu zī tōng cháng xī wàng yǒu wěn dìng de cháng qī bào chóu

Investing
Portfolio
Chinese: 投資組合
Pinyin: tóu zī zǔ hé

Explanation: A portfolio is the complete collection of investments that a person or institution holds — stocks, bonds, cash, real estate, and more.

Example: Diversifying your investments makes an investment portfolio more balanced.

中文例句: 分散投資可以讓投資組合更平衡。

fēn sàn tóu zī kě yǐ ràng tóu zī zǔ hé gèng píng héng

Quick Memory: Stock = ownership, Bond = lending, Return = the result, Risk = the uncertainty, Portfolio = the whole collection.
